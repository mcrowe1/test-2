Dawnland Prototype

Role: Lead designer/developer

Role

My responsibilities included designing and developing the prototype website. During the development, I communicated with the client and project manager including email correspondence and arranging video conference calls. I also created a “scope of work” document outlining required tasks for the second phase of the project once the prototype was finished.

Problem

The Upstander project needed a functioning website prototype to promote their documentary film “Dawnland”. The film deals with the oppression of Native American people and suppression of their culture throughout U.S history. The prototype conceptualizes an interactive website that educators and students could use to initiate discussion of Native American culture as it relates to both US history and current events. The goal was for the attendees of the 2017 Upstander Academy “Dawnland” event to test the prototype and offer feedback.

Research

The Upstander Project wanted to make the prototype fully functioning to enable access to Academy attendees. In using the prototype, the goal was for the attendees to collaborate and suggest additional interactive content for the finished site. In terms of the prototype itself, Upstander wanted to showcase the Dawnland trailer as well as have content/lessons for educators to use with their students. After researching various film and educational sites, I chose “I am Sacha” (http://www.iamsacha.nl/) and “Khan Academy” (https://www.khanacademy.org/) as a guideline for the prototype design. The prototype was designed to mirror the visual appeal of I Am Sacha and the simplicity of Khan Academy.

The home page showcases the Dawnland trailer as the main focus on the screen in order to engage the user right away. The secondary focus is on the link to the “Teacher's Guide” page to create an easy access to the educational content of the site.

The “Teacher's Guide” page is also the landing page for all the inquiry sections included in the teacher's guide. It contains a fixed sidebar that helps the user navigate between sections on the page.

The inquiry pages also contain a fixed sidebar that highlights the section that the user is currently on. The secondary navigation allows the user to access different features of each section more easily by organizing them into separate tabs (i.e all quotes, all actives).

User Testing

While using the prototype at the 2017 Upstander Academy, users provided feedback on the design and functionality. The main issue noted was that the sidebar was not fully responsive on tablet sized screens resulting in links being cut off from view (thereby inaccessible to users). Another commonly shared critique was that the secondary navigation on the inquiry pages should be fixed when the user scrolls down the page. Having a fixed navigation would eliminate the user having to scroll all the way to the top of the page to access the secondary navigation. Apart from those, most users found the prototype easy to use and visually pleasing.

Project Takeaway

What did I learn from this project? I gained more experience working remotely on a development team and with a client. Given a strict deadline, I was required to work efficiently to complete the various deliverables for the project. Looking back, I would have spent more time with the research process putting more emphasis on user research, to create a better experience for the intended user. Also, creating user stories tailored to the hierarchy of the user's goals and needs would have helped the wireframing process and overall construction of the prototype.

Chloe Productions

Role: Lead designer/developer

View PDF

Role

I had the opportunity to work remotely with a project manager based out of Montreal, Canada. Responsible for re-designing and developing the Chloe Productions site, I also set up conference calls and responded to various emails regarding different deliverables.

Problem

In promoting new business, Chloe Productions needed to redesign their website to not only match current industry standards, but to also stand out from the rest. While maintaining the original branding, the new site design had to be clean and modern with a young and exciting feel to it.The underlying requirement was that the new site had to be built using Wordpress.com.

Original site

Research

For this project, the majority of the research process consisted of browsing through various Wordpress.com themes as a foundation for the site. When looking through these themes, one of the key features I looked for was the lettering “menu” button on mobile as opposed to the popular hamburger menu button. Having this feature would be more user friendly for the Chloe Productions clientele who might not be accustomed to finding what they need behind three bars on mobile. Ultimately I decided on Business Identity, a premium Wordpress.com theme developed by We Cobble.

The original site mockups were created based off of the low fidelity sketches drawn above. Having the large hero image on the homepage is a common industry trend that creates an exciting visual appeal. All of the colors and fonts from the old site are used in this original mock up to stay true to the Chloe Productions branding.

View full mockup

Complications

Using the Business Identity theme on Wordpress.com created many problems when trying to implement the first mockup design. The functionality and design of the Wordpress.com theme made it very difficult to stick to the design I had created. The other problem was that I had designed the mockups before reviewing the revised content for the new site. I initially thought I could get away with using lorem ipsum and placeholder images to conceptualize what the new content might look like (based on the old site), but this proved to be very inefficient as the new changes were fairly significant. My solution moving forward was to redesign the whole site based on the Business Identity theme and the revised content. With the new direction of the project, I also decided to get rid of unnecessary sections and pages to create more focus around the Chloe Productions business model.

The new design creates more focus around the overall message of what Chloe Productions does as a company. The design matches the new content while keeping a clean and exciting look, and maintaining the original branding. Although there are fewer images compared to the previous design, I created multiple icons to contribute to the visual appeal and simplicity of the site.

Project Takeaway

Although I gained even more experience working remotely on a team and with a client, there are many things I would do differently when reviewing this project. The first would be to not rush the design process and spend more time researching a theme that is more tailored to the content of the site. I would also do more research into the target market of Chloe Productions in order to design the site according to the user's goals and needs rather than solely focusing on the Chloe Productions business model.
